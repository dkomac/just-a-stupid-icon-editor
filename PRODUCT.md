# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Logo Creator is for non-designers who want to make clean logos for projects, products, avatars, small brands, prototypes, and social profiles. They should be able to make something usable without knowing design tooling, while still having access to exact values when they need a shape to be centered, sized, colored, aligned, masked, or exported precisely.

## Product Purpose

Logo Creator is a focused browser-based logo editor. It provides a calm single-screen workspace for composing vector shapes, text, colors, masks, and layers, then exporting the result to SVG, JPG, PDF, or WebM.

Success means a user can quickly create a simple mark, adjust it visually, refine it with exact controls, manage layers without confusion, and export production-ready files without leaving the app.

## Brand Personality

Clean, calm, capable. The product should feel approachable before it feels powerful, but it should never hide important controls from users who want precision.

## Anti-references

The app should not feel like a dense professional design suite, a template-heavy logo generator, a playful toy editor, or a marketing landing page. Avoid crowded panels, decorative UI, gimmicky presets, and controls that require design vocabulary before they make sense.

## Design Principles

- Make the first action obvious: adding a shape, selecting it, changing color, and exporting should be immediately visible.
- Keep precision available, not dominant: exact controls belong in a clear inspector panel that appears when they are relevant.
- Treat SVG as the source of truth: logos are vector objects first, with raster and document exports derived from that model.
- Make layers understandable: order, visibility, lock state, duplication, deletion, renaming, and masking should be explicit.
- Favor confidence over cleverness: every control should behave predictably and make the canvas feel stable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The interface should target readable contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-reachable controls, reduced-motion-safe interactions, and shape/color workflows that do not rely on color alone.
